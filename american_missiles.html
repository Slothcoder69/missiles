American Missiles

1. Minuteman III

The Minuteman III is an intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) developed by the United States. It is a crucial element of the U.S. nuclear triad, designed to deliver nuclear warheads with high accuracy. The Minuteman III can reach speeds of Mach 23 (approximately 28,300 km/h or 17,600 mph) and has a range of about 13,000 km (8,080 miles).

Originally deployed in the 1970s, the Minuteman III has undergone several upgrades to enhance its reliability, accuracy, and survivability. The missile is housed in underground silos and is maintained by the United States Air Force. Its primary role is to provide a deterrent against potential nuclear threats and to ensure a credible second-strike capability.

2. Trident II (D5)

The Trident II (D5) is a submarine-launched ballistic missile (SLBM) deployed by the United States Navy. It is a vital component of the U.S. nuclear deterrent and is carried by Ohio-class submarines. The Trident II is capable of reaching speeds of Mach 24 (approximately 29,400 km/h or 18,270 mph) and has a range of about 12,000 km (7,456 miles).

This missile can carry multiple independently targetable reentry vehicles (MIRVs), allowing it to strike multiple targets across a wide area. The Trident II is known for its accuracy and reliability, making it a crucial element of U.S. strategic deterrence. It has been in service since the 1990s and continues to be a key component of the U.S. Navy's strategic capabilities.

3. AGM-86 ALCM

The AGM-86 Air-Launched Cruise Missile (ALCM) is a strategic cruise missile used by the United States Air Force. It can be launched from various bomber aircraft, including the B-52 Stratofortress. The AGM-86 ALCM is capable of flying at low altitudes to evade radar detection and can reach speeds of approximately Mach 0.8 (around 1,000 km/h or 621 mph).

With a range of about 2,400 km (1,500 miles), the AGM-86 ALCM is equipped with conventional or nuclear warheads. It serves as a versatile weapon for the U.S. military, providing options for precision strikes against high-value targets. The missile has been in service since the 1980s and has undergone upgrades to enhance its capabilities and accuracy.

4. AGM-183A ARRW

The AGM-183A Air-Launched Rapid Response Weapon (ARRW) is a hypersonic missile currently under development by the United States. Designed to provide rapid strike capabilities against high-value targets, the ARRW aims to significantly reduce enemy response times.

While specific performance details are classified, it is expected that the ARRW will achieve speeds exceeding Mach 20 (approximately 24,500 km/h or 15,220 mph) and will have a range of around 1,600 km (994 miles). The development of the ARRW reflects the growing emphasis on hypersonic technologies in modern warfare, providing the U.S. military with an edge in rapid response and strike capabilities.

5. Long Range Stand Off Weapon (LRSO)

The Long Range Stand Off Weapon (LRSO) is another strategic missile system currently in development by the United States. This air-launched cruise missile is designed to replace the aging AGM-86 ALCM and is expected to offer enhanced capabilities and survivability.

The LRSO is designed to carry nuclear warheads and will provide the U.S. Air Force with a versatile and effective platform for long-range precision strikes. While specific details about its speed and range remain classified, the LRSO is expected to enhance the U.S. nuclear deterrent and ensure a credible response to potential threats.
