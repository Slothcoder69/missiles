The Latest Developed Missiles in 2024 Are

United States

  • Mako Multi-Mission Hypersonic Missile: Unveiled at Sea Air Space 2024, this missile is being pitched to the U.S. Navy for its speed, affordability, and targeting versatility.
  • Next Generation Interceptor (NGI): Selected by the Missile Defense Agency in April 2024, this interceptor is designed to protect the homeland from long-range ballistic missile threats.
  • Advanced Anti-Radiation Guided Missile – Extended Range (AARGM-ER): This is a major upgrade to the current AARGM and will be fitted to several aircraft.
  • Sentinel: The first Sentinel prototype is planned to conduct a test flight in FY24.

India

  • Agni-4: An intermediate-range ballistic missile that was successfully launched from ITR in Chandipur, Odisha on September 6, 2024.
  • Agni-Prime: A medium-range ballistic missile that was successfully launched from Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Island in Odisha in May 2024. The Agni-Prime is a two-stage missile with a dual navigation and guidance system.
  • Phase-II Ballistic Missile Defence System: A system that was successfully flight-tested on July 24, 2024. The test demonstrated India's ability to defend against ballistic missiles with a range of 5,000 km.
  • New Generation AKASH (AKASH-NG): A missile that was tested by the Indian DRDO in 2024.
  • Very Short Range Air Defence System (VSHORADS): A missile that was tested by the Indian DRDO in 2024.
  • Indigenous Technology Cruise Missile (ITCM): A missile that was tested by the Indian DRDO in 2024.
  • Supersonic Missile-Assisted Release of Torpedo (SMART): A missile that was tested by the Indian DRDO in 2024.
  • RudraM-II supersonic air-to-surface: A missile that was tested by the Indian DRDO in 2024.

Russia

  • Sarmat (Satan II): This is a heavy intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) designed to replace the Soviet-era SS-18. It is reported to have a range of over 18,000 kilometers and can carry multiple nuclear warheads.
  • Zircon hypersonic missile: This is a hypersonic glide vehicle that can be launched from land, sea, or air. It is designed to be difficult to intercept due to its high speed and maneuverability.
  • Kh-35U anti-ship missile: This is an updated version of the older Kh-35 anti-ship missile. It has a longer range and is claimed to be more difficult to detect and intercept.

China

  • Dongfeng-41 (DF-41): This intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) has a range of 12,000–15,000 kilometers (7,500–9,300 miles), which is long enough to reach the US mainland. It can carry multiple independently targetable reentry vehicles (MIRVs) and is believed to be the world's longest-range missile.
  • JL-3 (CSS-N-20): This submarine-launched ballistic missile (SLBM) has an estimated range of 10,000 kilometers, which is long enough to target the continental US from near the Chinese coast. It is likely MIRV-capable.
  • Microwave photonic radar: This new radar has a detection range of over 600 kilometers and can identify false targets. It can track 10 hypersonic missiles.
  • MBDA's VSHORAD missile: This supersonic, fire-and-forget missile is designed to target small unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), helicopters, and fighters. It can be carried by a person, integrated into vehicles with automated turrets, or used with MBDA's Sky Warden counter-UAV system.
  • Tomahawk cruise missiles: The U.S. and U.K. Navies used Tomahawk missiles in 2024 to attack Houthi rebel sites in Yemen.
  • LMM missiles: The UK announced a £162 million package of LMM missiles to help increase UK and European defense production. The missiles are built by Thales and can be fired from a variety of platforms on land, sea, and air.
